Chenglong Wang

PhD candidate, Northeastern University (Shenyang, China)
Email / Google Scholar / GitHub

I am a Ph.D student of the 2021 cohort at the Natural Language Processing Laboratory of Northeastern University (co-advisors: Prof. Tong Xiao and Prof. Jingbo Zhu). My research focuses on language model alignment, with current interests including: (1) improving the training efficiency of large language models through reinforcement learning; (2) developing and training reward models; and (3) adapting RL-based methods to non-LLM paradigms such as multimodal large language models and diffusion large language models. In addition, I have participated in several machine translation workshops, including WGT (2020), QE (2020), and WMT (Efficiency and Translation tasks, 2021).

Select Open-Source Projects

Vision-LLM-Alignment
It is an alignment system that provides complete implementations of SFT, RLHF, and DPO for vision-based LLMs, including the LLaVA family and the LLaMA-3.2-Vision models. In this open-source release, we also provide a cleaned visual preference dataset.
DeepSpeed-Chat-Extension
This system extends DeepSpeed-Chat with additional modules for fine-tuning LLMs via SFT and RLHF/DPO. It integrates our hybrid alignment training framework as well as the ESRL method, both of which contribute to improved alignment performance in LLMs.
